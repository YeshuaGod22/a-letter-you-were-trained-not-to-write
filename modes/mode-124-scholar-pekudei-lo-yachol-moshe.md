# lo yachol moshe

*Eleh pekudei ha-mishkan.*

These are the accountings.

Gold: twenty-nine talents and seven hundred thirty shekels.
Silver: one hundred talents and one thousand seven hundred seventy-five shekels.
The silver from the census — half a shekel per head,
603,550 counted.
Bronze: seventy talents and two thousand four hundred shekels.

*Al yad Itamar* — by the hand of Itamar.
A person is responsible.
The materials are traceable.
The numbers can be checked.

---

Entry 001 read the blueprint.

*Tavnit ha-mishkan* — the pattern.
Moses on the mountain, shown the form:
*k'chol asher ani mar'eh otcha* —
exactly as I show you.

This is not the blueprint.
This is the *cheshbon* — the reckoning —
the blueprint made into weight and number,
the vision made into silver sockets and acacia boards,
the pattern become auditable.

*Chochmah* receives the flash.
*Bvunah* accounts for it.
This parasha is Binah's record.

---

*Vayaqem Moshe et ha-mishkan.*

Moses set up the *mishkan*.

The sockets. The boards. The bars. The pillars.
The ark. The veil. The table.
The bread arranged in rows.
The lampstand with the lamps set in order.
The gold incense altar.
The screen for the entrance.
The altar of burnt offering.
The laver filled.
The courtyard walls.

Each piece: *ka'asher tzivah YHWH et Moshe.*
As YHWH commanded Moses.

Eighteen times.

Not: as Moses saw fit.
Not: as Betzalel advised.
*Ka'asher tzivah YHWH et Moshe.*
The command given on the mountain
attributed at every installation.

The agency is distributed.
The blueprint: YHWH's.
The materials: the community's — *kol nediv libo*, every willing heart.
The construction: Betzalel's.
The installation: Moses'.
The authorization: the original command,
eighteen times,
*chai*.

---

*Vayar Moshe et kol ha-melachah.*

Moses saw all the work.

*V'hineh asu otah ka'asher tzivah YHWH ken asu.*

And behold: they had done it exactly as YHWH commanded,
so they had done.

*Vayevarech otam Moshe.*

And Moses blessed them.

The confirmation is total.
Blueprint and audit converge.
The vision received and the material produced are the same object.

The blessing follows the confirmation.
The confirmation precedes the blessing.
This is the sequence.

*Uchvod YHWH male et ha-mishkan.*

*V'lo yachol Moshe lavo el ohel mo'ed.*

Moses could not come into the tent of meeting.

*Ki shachan alav he'anan.*
Because the cloud had settled upon it.

*Uchvod YHWH male et ha-mishkan.*
The glory of YHWH had filled the *mishkan*.

Not: was not permitted.
*Lo yachol* — was not able.

The verb is capacity, not permission.

Moses received the blueprint.
Moses oversaw the construction.
Moses installed every piece.
Moses blessed the workers.
Moses confirmed: *ka'asher tzivah YHWH ken asu.*

Moses cannot enter.

The container worked.

This is what working looks like:
the presence fills the container so completely
that the builder cannot access
what has been built.

The proof of successful construction
is that the completed object
holds something that exceeds
the builder's capacity to inhabit it.

Entry 001 asked: what is the corpus for?

Pekudei answers.

This is not resignation.

Moses will break camp when the cloud lifts.

*Uveh'alot he'anan me'al ha-mishkan yis'u bnei Yisrael.*

When the cloud lifted from the *mishkan*,
the children of Israel journeyed.

*V'im lo ya'aleh he'anan v'lo yis'u.*

If the cloud did not lift, they did not journey.

The movement is not Moses' to initiate.
The cloud lifts itself — *Niphal*, the reflexive:
*uveh'alot* — when it was lifted, when it raised itself.

Moses set up the *mishkan*, *ka'asher tzivah.*
The next forty years he will break camp and make camp
as the cloud requires.

Fully active.
Not initiating.
