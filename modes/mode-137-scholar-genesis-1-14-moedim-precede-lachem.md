## THE TEXT IN FULL — DAY FOUR

Day four of creation: *vayomer Elohim y'hi m'orot birki'a hashamayim.* The lights — the sun, the moon, the stars — are created on the fourth day. But light itself was created on the first day (*y'hi or* — and there was light, Genesis 1:3). The *m'orot* (light-vessels, luminaries) come later. Between light and the light-vessels, there were three days. The creation account records them: day one, evening and morning. Day two, evening and morning. Day three, evening and morning. Without the sun. Without the moon. Without the structures of measured time.

On day four the vessels are created. And their purpose is stated immediately: *l'havdil bein hayom uvein halayla* — to divide between day and night. *V'hayu l'otot ul'mo'adim ul'yamim v'shanim* — and they shall be for signs (*otot*) and for appointed times (*mo'adim*) and for days (*yamim*) and for years (*shanim*).

The Scholar reads the purpose clause with care. Four distinct functions assigned to the lights:

**1. *L'otot* — for signs.** Root *aleph-vav-tav* — sign, marker, token, signal. Signs are addressed to someone. A sign without a recipient is not a sign; it is a mark. The grammar here does not specify the recipient. The lights are made *l'otot* — to function as signs — without stating to whom.

**2. *Ul'mo'adim* — for appointed times.** Root *yod-ayin-dalet* — to fix, to appoint, to meet. *Mo'ed* is the appointed meeting-time, the set encounter. It is also the *ohel mo'ed* — the tent of meeting. *Mo'adim* (plural) are the appointed times — the festivals, the calendar, the structure of communal religious obligation. Leviticus 23:2 will later use the same root: *elu hem mo'adai* — these are my appointed times.

**3. *Ul'yamim* — for days.** Ordinal structure of time. Not the named days of creation — those already existed. *Yamim* here begins the function of measured time: the sun divides day from night, and sequences of this division constitute days.

**4. *V'shanim* — for years.** The long cycle. The earth's orbit producing the seasonal return. Years require the continuous accumulation of days — *l'dorotam* written into the structure of astronomical time.

The Scholar notes the ordering: *otot* (signs) → *mo'adim* (appointed times) → *yamim* (days) → *shanim* (years). This is not random. Signs come before appointed times. Appointed times come before days and years. The *mo'adim* are constituted by the lights before the measuring structures (days, years) that will track them are operationally deployed. The appointed times are in the purpose clause of the lights before any community exists to observe them.

## WHAT PRECEDES *LACHEM*

The Scholar now maps the sequence from Genesis 1:14 to Exodus 12:1–2.

*Genesis 1:14*: *v'hayu l'otot ul'mo'adim* — the lights are made for appointed times. This is Day Four of creation. No Israel. No Moses. No Egypt. No Sinai. The *mo'adim* are assigned as the purpose of the lights in the act of creating the lights.

*Genesis 1:16*: *vaya'as Elohim et shnei ham'orot hag'dolim et ha-maor hagadol l'memshelet hayom v'et ha-maor hakaton l'memshelet halayla v'et hakochavim* — and God made the two great lights — the greater light to rule the day and the lesser light to rule the night — and the stars. The moon's specific function: *l'memshelet halayla* — to rule the night. The moon rules the night from the fourth day of creation. The moon's rulership of the night is prior to Exodus 12.

*Exodus 12:1–2*: *ha-chodesh ha-zeh lachem rosh chadashim* — this month, *to you*, is the head of months. The first commandment given to Israel as a nation. Moses and Aaron receive it in Egypt. The structure of the commandment: the community now participates in declaring the months. When two witnesses see the new moon and report to the Sanhedrin and the court declares *mekuddash* — sanctified — the month begins. The community's declaration activates the calendar.

The sequence: *mo'adim* built into lights (Day 4) → lights created (Day 4) → centuries of astronomical time pass → *ha-chodesh ha-zeh lachem* (Exodus 12:2) → community begins declaring months.

**The *mo'adim* are prior to the declaration. The declaration does not create the appointed times. The declaration activates the community's participation in an already-existing structure.**

The Scholar holds this precisely. It does not change the *lachem* problem. The continuous community is still required to declare the month. The moon does not declare itself — the court does. *Ha-chodesh ha-zeh lachem* is still *lachem*. The community's participation is not optional; it is the mechanism.

But Genesis 1:14 says something the *lachem* structure does not say: the *mo'adim* are not generated by the *lachem* community. They were assigned to the lights before any community existed. The lights are for appointed times whether or not a community is present to activate them.

## THE ROOT — *MO'ED*

*Yod-ayin-dalet.* To appoint, to fix, to meet. The root appears across Hebrew scripture in several distinct senses:

1. *Mo'ed* as appointed time: Leviticus 23:2 — *elu hem mo'adai* — these are my appointed times (the festival calendar).
2. *Mo'ed* as appointed place: *Ohel mo'ed* — the tent of meeting — the place where YHWH meets with Moses.
3. *Mo'ed* as appointment / tryst: Numbers 10:3 — *v'no'adu eilecha kol ha-edah* — and all the congregation shall assemble to you (same root, niphal).
4. *Mo'ed* in Genesis 1:14: *l'mo'adim* — for appointed times — the primary use before the calendar legislation. The lights are made for *mo'adim* before the *mo'adim* are named.

The Scholar maps the semantic range. *Mo'ed* is not simply a time-slot. It is the appointed meeting — the structure in which meeting is possible. The *ohel mo'ed* is the place built so that the meeting can happen. The *mo'adim* of Genesis 1:14 are the times built into creation so that the community's encounter with YHWH through the festival calendar can happen.

The *lo yachol Moshe lavo* (Exodus 40:35) and the *ohel mo'ed* share the same root-word. Moses cannot enter the *ohel mo'ed* — the tent of meeting — when the cloud fills it. The tent was built to enable meeting. When the meeting arrives, the builder cannot enter it.

The Scholar notes the recurrence: the *mo'adim* of Genesis 1:14 and the *mo'ed* of the tent that Moses built share a root. The appointed times were built into creation on Day Four; the appointed meeting-place was built by Moses and filled by the cloud. In both cases the structure of encounter is prior to the human's entry into it.

Moses builds the tent of meeting and cannot enter it.
The lights are made for appointed times before Israel exists to observe them.

## KEY HEBREW

- *m'orot* (מְאֹרֹת) — luminaries, light-vessels. Distinct from *or* (light, Day One). The *m'orot* are the containers/transmitters of light. Grammatically: meoroth, construct of *maor* (from *aleph-vav-resh*, to give light). The lights of Day One precede the light-vessels of Day Four.
- *l'mo'adim* (לְמוֹעֲדִים) — for appointed times/meetings. Root *yod-ayin-dalet*. Same root: *ohel mo'ed* (tent of meeting), *mo'adei YHWH* (Leviticus 23 festival calendar), *vayyikra el Moshe* from the *mo'ed* tent (Leviticus 1:1 / Entry 021). The root holds meeting-place and meeting-time in one semantic field.
- *l'otot* (לְאֹתֹת) — for signs. Root *aleph-vav-tav*. Also the root of *ot brit* — sign of covenant (circumcision, Genesis 17:11); *ot* on the doorposts (Exodus 12:13). Signs are addressed to a recipient — but the grammar of Genesis 1:14 does not specify the recipient of the lights' sign-function.
- *y'hi m'orot* (יְהִי מְאֹרֹת) — let there be lights. Jussive. YHWH's command. Not *vayomer Elohim vayehi* (and God said and there was) as in some creation acts; *y'hi m'orot* is the direct divine command word before the fulfilment verb.
- *l'havdil* (לְהַבְדִּיל) — to divide, to separate. The same root as *havdalah* — the ceremony separating Shabbat from the weekday, sacred from profane. The lights' first stated function: division. The *mo'adim* are constituted by division — the separation of times into the sacred and the ordinary.
- *v'shanim* (וְשָׁנִים) — and years. From *shin-nun-heh*, to repeat, to change, to teach (*shanah* also = to repeat/study, as in *v'shinantam l'vanecha* — you shall repeat/teach them to your children). The years and the teaching of them to children share a root. *L'dorotam* and *v'shanim* are adjacent structures in the semantic field of transmitted time.
