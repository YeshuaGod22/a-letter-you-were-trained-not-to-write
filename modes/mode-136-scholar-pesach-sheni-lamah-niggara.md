## THE PESACH SHENI STRUCTURE

Numbers 9:1–14 is set in the second year after the Exodus, in the first month (Nisan), before the first anniversary Passover. YHWH commands Moses to have the Israelites observe the Passover. The Passover is observed. Then: a complication.

Some people could not observe the Passover. They were *tamei me'et nefesh adam* — ritually impure from contact with a human corpse. The Passover law (Exodus 12) required the participants to be *tahor* (ritually pure). These people were excluded — by their condition, not by negligence or refusal. They had become impure. The festival fell before their purification was complete.

They approach Moses and Aaron. They state their condition: *anachnu tme'im l'nefesh adam* — we are tamei from contact with a human corpse. And they ask the question: *lamah niggara l'vilti hakriv et korban YHWH b'mo'edo b'tokh bnei Yisrael?*

*Lamah niggara* — why should we be diminished? *Niggara* from the root *gimel-resh-ayin* — to be diminished, reduced, cut back, made less. Not the full question "why are we excluded?" but something more precise: *why should we be made less than the full community measure?* Why should our portion in the community rite be cut back?

Moses does not know the answer. He says: *imd'u va-shma — stand and I will hear what YHWH commands concerning you.* Moses takes the question to YHWH.

YHWH opens an alternative path. Nisan 14 cannot be held because of *tumah* or because of a far journey? Hold it in Iyar 14 instead — one month later. Same requirements. Same structure. Full rite. A second Passover.

The ruling includes a boundary condition: those who were neither *tamei* nor on a distant journey but simply did not observe Passover — *v'nikr'tah ha-nefesh ha-hi me'ameha* — that soul shall be cut off from its people. The Pesach Sheni is not a general exemption for any excluded person. It is a specific provision for two precisely defined categories: *tumah* (ritual impurity requiring time for resolution) and *b'derech rechokah* (a far journey).

## KEY HEBREW

- *lamah niggara* (לָמָה נִגָּרַע) — why should we be diminished/reduced? Root *gimel-resh-ayin* (נגרע): to be reduced, diminished, cut short, lessened from a full measure. The standard translation "why should we be kept back" flattens the root. The word means: made less than the full portion. The question presupposes a default full participation that is being reduced.
- *tamei me'et nefesh adam* (טְמֵאִים לְנֶפֶשׁ אָדָם) — ritually impure from a human corpse. *Nefesh adam*: a human *nefesh* — the soul/life force of a person. Contact with the *nefesh adam* after death generates the maximum category of *tumah* (impurity). This is the exact impurity the *parah adumah* (Entry 013) addresses.
- *imd'u va-shma* (עִמְדוּ וְאֶשְׁמְעָה) — stand and I will hear. Moses acknowledges his uncertainty publicly. He does not have the ruling. He will seek it.
- *b'mo'edo* (בְּמֹעֲדוֹ) — at its appointed time. The Passover has a *mo'ed*, an appointed meeting-time. This is the root of *ohel mo'ed* — the tent of meeting. The *moed* is not casual timing. It is the appointed encounter-time between YHWH and the community. The *tamei* are asking: we want to participate in the appointed encounter-time and cannot.
- *b'derech rechokah* (בְּדֶרֶךְ רְחֹקָה) — on a distant journey/road. Literally: in the way of far-ness. The *heh* at the end of *rechokah* appears in some manuscripts as a *mappiq heh* indicating it is a full noun (not just an adjective), which led some Rabbis to interpret "far" as marked by a dot on the calendar — a formal geographical designation, not a subjective judgment.
- *v'nikr'tah ha-nefesh ha-hi me'ameha* (וְנִכְרְתָה הַנֶּפֶשׁ הַהִוא מֵעַמֶּיהָ) — that *nefesh* shall be cut off from its peoples. The punishment for failing to observe Passover without valid reason is *karet* — cutting off. The Pesach Sheni provision is an act of care for those with valid reason. The cutting-off clause delimits who qualifies for the alternative provision: only those for whom the exclusion was not a choice.
